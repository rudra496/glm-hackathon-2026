# Code of Conduct

**AI for Social Good Hackathon – SUST 2026**

We are committed to making this event welcoming, respectful, and safe for everyone — regardless of department, academic year, gender, background, or experience level.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior
- Treat all participants, judges, volunteers, and organizers with respect.
- Support beginners; encourage learning and teamwork.
- Give proper credit for any tools, libraries, or ideas used from others.
- Engage constructively in discussions and feedback.
- Follow all event rules and submission guidelines.

### Unacceptable Behavior
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Hate speech, offensive language, or abusive conduct.
- Cheating, plagiarism, or sabotaging another team's work.
- Sharing private or sensitive information without consent.
- Misrepresenting AI usage, authorship, or team composition.

---

## Reporting

If you witness or experience a violation, please report it privately to the organizer:

- **Contact:** Rudra Sarker (Organizer, RoboSUST Club)
- **Method:** In person during the event, or via official contact channels

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and respect.

---

## Enforcement

Organizers may take any of the following actions:

1. **Warning** – Verbal or written notice of the violation.
2. **Penalty** – Reduction in submission eligibility or score consideration.
3. **Disqualification** – Removal from the event with forfeiture of prizes.

Organizer and judge decisions on enforcement are final.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces associated with the hackathon — the venue, this repository, any communication channels used during the event, and public representations of the event.

---

*This Code of Conduct is adapted from community standards used by open-source and university hackathon communities.*
