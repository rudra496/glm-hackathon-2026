# Participation Rules

**AI for Social Good Hackathon – SUST 2026**

---

## 1. Eligibility

- Open to **all currently enrolled students at SUST** — all departments and academic years are welcome.
- Team size: **2–4 members** (solo participation requires organizer approval).
- Each participant may be part of only one team.
- Beginners are welcome; no prior AI experience is required.

---

## 2. What to Build

- AI-powered projects targeting **social good** — education, healthcare, inclusion, environment, or campus life.
- Projects must be functional at prototype level by the submission deadline.
- Allowed types: web apps, mobile apps, bots, automation tools, research prototypes, or productivity systems.

---

## 3. Build Window

- All core development must take place **during event hours**.
- Prior learning, research, and environment setup are allowed before event day.
- Reusing open-source libraries is allowed **with proper attribution**.
- Reusing existing finished personal or team projects is **not allowed**.

---

## 4. Z.ai Usage Requirement

- Every project must **meaningfully integrate Z.ai tools and/or APIs** — not just superficially.
- Teams must document where and how Z.ai is used in their submission.
- Starter kits using Z.ai are provided in [`starter-kits/`](../starter-kits/).

---

## 5. Required Deliverables

Each team must submit the following before the deadline:

1. Public GitHub repository with working code
2. `README.md` in their repo: problem statement, solution, setup instructions, tech stack, team info
3. Z.ai usage explanation
4. Demo video or live demo link (2–5 minutes)
5. Completed submission form (link TBD)

---

## 6. Ethics & Safety

- No harmful, abusive, discriminatory, or illegal content or use cases.
- Respect user privacy — avoid exposing sensitive or personal data.
- Disclose all AI tools and significant AI-generated code used.

---

## 7. Disqualification Conditions

Teams may be disqualified for:

- Plagiarism or substantial copying of code without attribution.
- Submitting non-functional or unrelated repositories.
- False claims about authorship, AI usage, or team composition.
- Violating the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- Missing the final submission deadline.

---

## 8. Judging & Decisions

- Scoring follows the rubric in [`docs/JUDGING_RUBRIC.md`](./JUDGING_RUBRIC.md).
- Judges may request clarification from teams.
- Organizer and judge decisions are final.

---

## 9. Intellectual Property

- Teams retain full ownership of their code.
- By participating, teams grant organizers permission to mention team names, project titles, and demo links in non-commercial event reporting (social media, documentation, etc.).

---

## 10. Code of Conduct

All participants must follow the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) throughout the event.
