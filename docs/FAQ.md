# Frequently Asked Questions

**AI for Social Good Hackathon – SUST 2026**

---

## For Participants

### Who can join?
All currently enrolled students at **Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST)** — from any department or academic year. CSE, SWE, EEE, Civil, Business, and all other departments are equally welcome.

### Do I need prior AI or coding experience?
No. This hackathon is beginner-friendly. Starter kits, onboarding sessions, and Z.ai tool tutorials are provided on event day. Come ready to learn!

### Is there a registration fee?
No. This is a **zero-cost** event. Participation is completely free.

### How do I register?
Registration details and the form link will be announced closer to the event date. Watch RoboSUST Club channels and this repository for updates.

### Can I participate solo?
Solo participation is possible but requires **organizer approval**. Teams of 2–4 are preferred for a better collaborative experience.

### Is Z.ai usage mandatory?
Yes. Every project must meaningfully integrate **Z.ai tools or APIs**. Starter kits using Z.ai are provided to help you get started quickly.

### Can we use open-source libraries or external templates?
Yes — with proper attribution. You must clearly document external tools, libraries, and AI-generated content in your submission README.

### Can I use AI coding tools like GitHub Copilot or ChatGPT to write code?
Yes. AI-assisted development is allowed. However, your team remains fully responsible for the correctness, originality, and licensing of your code. Disclose significant AI usage in your project README.

### Can we use frameworks and APIs other than Z.ai?
Yes. You are not limited to Z.ai only, but Z.ai integration must be present and meaningful.

### How long is the event?
The hackathon runs for **one full day**. See the schedule in the main [README](../README.md) for the indicative timeline.

### What should we bring?
- Laptop and charger
- Student ID
- Your team members
- Your ideas!

### When is the submission deadline?
The exact deadline will be confirmed on event day. Typically, submissions close in the late afternoon during the submission window. Late submissions may be penalized.

### How do we submit?
Fill in the submission form (link TBD), and ensure your public GitHub repository is ready with all required files. See [`docs/SUBMISSION.md`](./SUBMISSION.md) for full details.

### What if our project is not fully finished?
Submit what you have before the deadline. Partially complete projects are still evaluated. Focus on demonstrating the core idea and Z.ai integration.

### Will we get certificates?
Yes. All participants who complete a valid submission receive a **digital certificate of participation**. Top teams receive certificates of achievement.

[Z.ai](https://z.ai) is an AI platform that provides powerful AI capabilities through accessible APIs and tools. The hackathon is supported by Z.ai to help participants build real AI projects without cost barriers.

## For Organizers & Volunteers

### Can I reuse this repository for future hackathons?
Yes. Update the event-specific details (date, venue, sponsor, tracks) and this repository is ready for the next edition.

### Where is the organizer checklist?
See [`docs/ORGANIZER_CHECKLIST.md`](./ORGANIZER_CHECKLIST.md) for a complete pre-event and day-of checklist.

### How do I enable the GitHub Pages website?
1. Go to **Settings → Pages** in your repository.
2. Under Source, select **Deploy from a branch**.
3. Choose `main` branch and `/docs` folder.
4. Save — your site will be live within minutes.

### Who do I contact with questions?
Reach out to the organizer, **Rudra Sarker**, through RoboSUST Club official channels.
